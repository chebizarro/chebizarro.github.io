This is a working notebook — half essay collection, half laboratory
log. I keep it because thinking in public is one of the few tools that
reliably improves the work. Writing forces ideas to hold their shape.

## What lives here

Three kinds of material:

- **Writing** — shorter observations, notes on tools, working memos.
- **Research** — longer essays intended to hold up over time. Pieces
  marked *draft* are exactly that: the argument is real, the prose is
  still settling.
- **Systems** — the software I actually ship, with the reasoning
  behind it and the lessons it cost.

## A confession, for the record

The first version of this site was scaffolded by an AI from a design
prompt, and the model — never one to let an empty page win — invented
an entire body of work for me: fictional projects, fictional talks, a
fictional CV with tastefully vague employers. It was flattering,
plausible, and wrong in every particular. Which is to say, it made
the case for this site's editorial policy better than I could have.

So, the policy: everything here describes systems that exist and runs
I actually made. Where models helped me think or draft, I say so.
Where I am uncertain, I say so. Corrections arrive as amendments, not
silent edits.

> The goal is not to be interesting. The goal is to be *correct*, and
> then, if you can manage it, clear.

That is the whole editorial policy. The irony of it having been
necessary is not lost on me.
